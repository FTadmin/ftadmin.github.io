Frequently Asked Questions

What is a normal blood pressure reading?

According to medical guidelines, normal blood pressure is typically below 120/80 mmHg. Readings between 120-129 systolic and below 80 diastolic are considered elevated. Values of 130/80 or higher may indicate hypertension. However, target ranges can vary by age and health conditions, so consult your doctor for personalized guidance.

How does OCR scanning work?

Simply point your iPhone or iPad camera at your blood pressure monitor's digital display. The app uses advanced OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology powered by AI to automatically read the numbers and populate the systolic, diastolic, and pulse fields. This eliminates manual typing errors and makes logging faster.

What is MAP and should I track it?

MAP (Mean Arterial Pressure) is a calculated value that represents the average blood pressure in your arteries during one cardiac cycle. It's calculated as: MAP = (2 √ó Diastolic + Systolic) √∑ 3. Some doctors find MAP useful for assessing cardiovascular risk. The app automatically calculates MAP for each reading.

Can I import my old blood pressure data?

Yes! If you've been tracking blood pressure in Apple Health, you can import all your historical readings into the app with one tap. The app also supports importing from CSV/Excel files if you have data from other sources.

How often should I check my blood pressure?

This depends on your health condition and your doctor's recommendations. Some people check daily (often morning and evening), while others check weekly. The app makes it easy to track as frequently as recommended by your healthcare provider. You can set reminders to help maintain a consistent schedule.

Is my blood pressure data private?

Absolutely. Your blood pressure readings are stored encrypted on your device and in your private iCloud account. The data never passes through our servers, and we cannot see your health information. It's as secure as Apple's Notes or Photos apps.

Can I track blood pressure for family members?

The app is designed for individual use and syncs with your personal iCloud account. For tracking multiple family members, each person should use the app on their own device with their own Apple ID.

Do I need a subscription?
