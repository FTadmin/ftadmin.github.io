How Sleep & Dreams Works

A complete sleep tracking solution to help you understand and improve your rest

1. Log Sleep Times

Quick Entry: Each morning, enter when you went to bed and when you woke up. The app automatically calculates your total sleep duration, so you don't have to do the math.

Multiple Entries: Track naps and multiple sleep sessions per day. Perfect for shift workers, parents with interrupted sleep, or anyone with non-traditional sleep schedules.

Detailed Notes: Record sleep quality ratings, dreams, nightmares, how you felt waking up, and any factors that affected your rest - caffeine intake, exercise timing, room temperature, stress levels, screen time before bed, or medications. The more details you add, the better AI can identify patterns.

2. Review Patterns

Calendar View: See your entire sleep history in an intuitive calendar interface. Each day displays sleep duration with color-coded indicators showing whether you met your sleep goals.

Sleep Statistics: Automatically calculated metrics include average sleep duration, total sleep time per week, sleep deficit/surplus, most consistent sleep schedule days, and trends over time. Identify your best and worst nights at a glance.

Visual Trends: Charts show your sleep patterns over days, weeks, or months. See how your bedtime and wake time vary, spot irregular patterns, and track progress toward consistent sleep schedules.

3. Get AI Insights

Pattern Recognition: AI analyzes your notes to identify what affects your sleep. Discover correlations like "You sleep 45 minutes longer on nights when you exercise" or "Sleep quality is worse when you have caffeine after 2 PM."

Personalized Tips: Get specific recommendations based on your data. AI might suggest optimal bedtimes based on when you consistently get the best sleep, or highlight factors that repeatedly disrupt your rest.

Sleep Chat: Ask natural language questions about your sleep data: "How many hours did I average last week?" or "Show me all nights where I got less than 6 hours." Get instant, conversational answers from your sleep history.

4. Improve Your Sleep

Actionable Data: Use insights to make evidence-based changes to your routine. Test hypotheses like "Will reading before bed improve my sleep?" by tracking outcomes and letting AI spot the differences.

Professional Reports: Export PDF or Excel reports for sleep specialists, doctors, or CPAP therapy consultations. Reports include sleep duration graphs, quality trends, notes about sleep disruptions, and statistical summaries formatted for medical review.

Apple Health Sync: Integrate with Apple Health to combine manual logs with automatic sleep tracking from Apple Watch or other devices. Import historical sleep data or export your logs to HealthKit for a complete health picture.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much sleep do I need?

Most adults need 7-9 hours of sleep per night, though individual needs vary. The app helps you track your sleep duration and identify your personal optimal amount based on how you feel.

Does this app automatically track sleep?

No. Unlike Apple Watch or other wearables, Sleep Diary requires manual logging. You enter your sleep and wake times each morning. However, you can import sleep data from Apple Health if you use other automatic tracking devices.

Can I log naps?

Yes. You can log multiple sleep periods per day, including naps. Each entry shows duration, and the calendar displays all your sleep/wake times.

What's the difference between this and Apple Health?

Sleep Diary provides a dedicated interface for sleep tracking with features specifically designed for managing sleep patterns - notes, AI insights, trends, and easy manual logging. It integrates with Apple Health but offers more detailed sleep-focused features.

How does AI help with sleep insights?

When you add notes about your day (e.g., "had coffee at 8pm" or "exercised in the morning"), AI analyzes these over time to show correlations with your sleep duration and quality. This helps identify what promotes or disrupts good sleep.

Can I share sleep data with my doctor?

Yes. Export your sleep history as PDF or Excel reports to share with healthcare providers. This is particularly useful if you're being evaluated for sleep disorders or insomnia.

Does it track sleep quality or just duration?

The app primarily tracks sleep duration (hours slept) based on your logged sleep and wake times. You can add notes about sleep quality, dreams, or how rested you feel, which helps with qualitative tracking.

Is there a reminder to log sleep?

Yes. You can set daily reminders to log your sleep times. Many users set a morning reminder to prompt them to record the previous night's sleep.

Do you show ads or track me?

No. We've completely removed all advertisements from the app. We don't track you across other apps or websites, and we never sell your data to advertisers.

Your data syncs via iCloud - just like Apple Notes and Photos. This means we don't need to know your email address, name, or any personal information. Your sleep data is stored on your devices and in your iCloud account, and it's completely private whether you use the free version or premium.

Do I need a subscription?

The app is completely free to download and use. All core tracking features work without a subscription. A premium subscription unlocks advanced AI features, unlimited exports, and enhanced customization. You can try all premium features during the free trial period.
